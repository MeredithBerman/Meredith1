Introduction

Everyday Code

Almost everything we do and see in today’s technological world is due to the code behind the applications. Take TikTok, for example: if you like, comment, or share a TikTok about a certain topic, you are almost guaranteed to see more of that content. The code behind the app feeds you more of what it knows you're interested in, keeping you scrolling for hours. Because of this, it’s easy to only see one side of a story or live in a world filled with content that aligns solely with your preferences. A data visualization activity I completed brought attention to how much I use TikTok on a weekly basis—it was one of my most-used apps. This is because the app's code delivers endless amounts of content related to my interests. These types of addictive algorithms make me concerned for future generations, who will grow up with endless scrolling and unlimited screen time.

Code and digital technology impact on education

Comparing what and how I learn in school to what and how my parents learned reveals the drastic evolution of education in parallel with the growth of technology. What used to be taught on chalkboards is now displayed on screens in every classroom. Kids today have endless resources at their fingertips in the form of smartphones, laptops, computers, and more. This is a significant advantage over past generations, who couldn’t simply Google the answers to their questions. Today, code plays a crucial role in many, if not all, classrooms. I’ve been using personalized software since middle school. These applications assess what you know and provide examples and practice in areas where you need improvement. This type of focused learning algorithm has been extremely helpful for me and my classmates, making them valuable resources to take advantage of.
